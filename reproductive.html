Human Body

Reproductive System

Male Reproductive System

The male reproductive system is designed to produce, maintain, and transport sperm and protective fluid (semen), and to discharge sperm within the female reproductive tract during sex.

  • Testes: Produce sperm and testosterone, the primary male sex hormone.
  • Scrotum: A skin-covered sac that regulates the temperature of the testes.
  • Vas deferens: Transports mature sperm to the urethra.
  • Prostate gland: Secretes fluid that nourishes and protects sperm.
  • Penis: Delivers sperm into the female body during intercourse.

Female Reproductive System (External)

The external female reproductive organs, collectively known as the vulva, include structures that protect internal organs and support sexual function.

  • Labia majora and minora: Skin folds protecting internal genital organs.
  • Clitoris: A sensitive organ involved in sexual pleasure.
  • Urethral opening: Allows urine to exit the body.
  • Vaginal opening: Entrance to the vagina, important for menstruation, intercourse, and childbirth.

Female Reproductive System (Internal)

The internal organs are involved in producing eggs, fertilization, fetal development, and childbirth.

  • Ovaries: Produce eggs and female hormones.
  • Fallopian tubes: Where fertilization usually occurs.
  • Uterus: Site for fetal development.
  • Vagina: Birth canal and passage for menstrual flow.
  • Cervix: Connects uterus to the vagina, opens during childbirth.
