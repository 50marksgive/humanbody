Human Body

Introduction – Anup Bhattarai

The human body is an extraordinary biological machine composed of many complex systems that work in harmony to maintain life. These systems are made up of organs and tissues that carry out specific roles, such as transporting nutrients, regulating temperature, and reproducing. Understanding how these systems interact helps us appreciate how intricate and resilient the body is. Each system has unique functions, yet they are interconnected and rely on each other to perform optimally. From the brain that controls our thoughts to glands that regulate hormones, every part has a vital role in keeping us alive and healthy.

Fun Facts About the Human Body

  • The human body has 206 bones in adulthood.
  • The brain uses about 20% of the body’s energy.
