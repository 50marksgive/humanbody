Human Body

Glandular System

The glandular system, also known as the endocrine system, consists of glands that secrete hormones directly into the bloodstream. These hormones regulate metabolism, growth and development, tissue function, sexual function, reproduction, sleep, and mood, among other things.

This system works closely with the nervous system to maintain homeostasis. The major glands include the pituitary, thyroid, adrenal, pancreas, and gonads. Each gland releases specific hormones that target different organs and tissues.

  • Regulates long-term body processes
  • Maintains internal balance and stability
  • Interacts with the nervous system to control bodily functions
